Before you read
What is a setup

Every completed M5 candle where the market state is trending up, trending down, or breakout. That is one moment the bot could open a position. Over 14 days there were 1,361 setups across both symbols — about 97 per day.

What counts as a win

For each setup we look at the next 60 minutes of M5 candles. If price moved at least 1 ATR in the right direction — up for BUY, down for SELL — it is a win. This is a proxy for real trade outcome, not exact SL/TP, but it is consistent across all conditions.

Break-even win rate at 1.67 R:R (1.5× ATR stop, 2.5× ATR target) is 37.5%. Any strategy below that loses money long-term regardless of how it is managed.
Strategy v1 — Active filters
What the bot checks before opening a trade
Base signal regime ∈ {trending_up, trending_down, breakout}
The foundation. Every M5 candle where the indicator has classified an active trend or breakout. Without this there is no trade. This produced 1,361 possible entries over 14 days.
Session filter utc_hour BETWEEN 7 AND 21
Only trade during London and New York sessions. Blocks Tokyo hours (23:00–06:59 UTC). The assumption was that Tokyo has lower liquidity and noisier signals.
RSI zone BUY: RSI 50–60 · SELL: RSI 40–50
RSI must be in a tight momentum band. Above 50 confirms upward momentum for a BUY but below 60 so it is not already overbought. Same logic inverted for SELL. This is a very narrow window.
H1 ADX minimum h1_adx > 20
The H1 timeframe must show real trend strength. If H1 ADX is below 20, the H1 is basically flat, and any M5 trend signal might be noise going nowhere.
M15 gate M15 ranging → H1 must confirm
When M15 is ranging (consolidating), H1 must be trending in the same direction as the M5 signal. If M15 is not ranging, this gate does not apply. Prevents entering an M5 breakout inside a larger range that H1 does not support.

Findings
What the numbers actually mean
✗
Session filter is blocking good trades

Removing it adds 37 more trades and raises win rate from 44.6% to 47.2%. Those Tokyo-session setups that were being blocked are winning more often than London/NY ones. The assumption that Tokyo is noisier is not supported by this data.

≈
RSI zone adds little benefit but removing it floods trades

Dropping RSI gives 580 setups (vs 175 now) but win rate drops 1%. The narrow 50–60 / 40–50 window is selecting a slightly better subset but not enough to justify cutting 77% of entries. A wider band might perform better.

✓
M15 gate is earning its place

Removing it drops win rate by 2.4%. This is the one filter confirmed to add value inside the combined strategy. When M15 is ranging and H1 does not confirm the direction, those trades do worse. Keep this.

≈
H1 ADX > 20 is close to neutral

Removing it improves win rate by 0.6% and adds 46 more trades. Not a strong signal either way. It is not hurting badly but it is not helping much either.

↑
Volume confirmation genuinely helps

Adding volume above MA pushes win rate to 49.1% (+4.5%). The downside is it cuts trades from 175 to 53 — too small a sample to be certain. Promising, but needs more data before making it a required filter.

↑
Near S/R is the strongest signal found

Adding near S/R gives 54.3% win rate (+9.7%). Only 35 trades though — too small to be certain. Consistent with Test 1 result (60.6%). Worth tracking as data builds up.

!
The combined strategy underperforms the raw signal

The base M5 signal alone wins 51.7%. The current strategy with all filters wins 44.6%. The filters together are making it worse — still above the 37.5% breakeven, but the strategy is actively filtering out good trades.

Next — v2 candidates
What to test in the next version
1
Remove the session filter

The data says Tokyo setups within this strategy win more often, not less. Run without the session filter for 2–4 weeks and compare live results to this baseline.

2
Widen the RSI zone

Try 45–65 for BUY and 35–55 for SELL instead of the current tight 50–60 / 40–50. Re-run Test 1 and Test 2 and see if the wider window improves win rate without flooding bad trades.

3
Keep the M15 gate

This is the one filter that is confirmed to add value. Do not touch it in v2.

4
Watch volume and S/R as data grows

Both show strong signals but the sample is too small (35–53 trades) to make them required. Collect another month of data then re-run this exact same analysis on those conditions.

14 days and ~175 setups per combination is a small sample. Every percentage here has uncertainty. Treat these as direction, not proof. Re-run this analysis after every strategy change and after 4+ weeks of new clean data.
